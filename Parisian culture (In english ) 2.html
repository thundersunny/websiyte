Parisian culture is a blend of different cultures, values, beliefs, and social behavioral patterns that have mixed over time due to historical events and cross-cultural interactions. Modern Parisian culture includes different interdependent cultural groups that interact and influence each other significantly, leading to the creation of a unique Parisian culture known as the blending of European and Islamic cultures. The city is today one of the world's leading business and cultural centers, with a rich history of arts, music, museums, festivals, and other entertainment.



Paris' culinary reputation has its basis in the diverse regional origins of its inhabitants, and France's regions have regional varieties of wine. These mingled with Paris' own regional traditions, and in its beginnings, Paris' culinary development owe much to the 19th-century organization of a railway system that had Paris as a center, making the capital a focal point for migration from France's many different regions and gastronomical cultures. Parisians behave in ways that foreigners find hard to understand, and they are often perceived as aggressive, ill-mannered, and hostile. However, adjusting to the Parisian culture and behavior is possible, and it requires an understanding of the cultural norms and values that shape the city's social interactions .

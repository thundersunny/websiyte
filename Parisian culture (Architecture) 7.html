Paris is known for its unique and trend-setting role in the story of French architecture, displaying a fascinating multifaceted metropolis. The city has notable examples of architecture of every period, from the Middle Ages to the 21st century. Paris’ modern buildings have developed gradually out of earlier styles; palaces and mansions have survived by transforming into apartments and shops, and most streets harbor a range of buildings from various centuries.


Paris’ architecture is remarkable compared to other European cities, standing out for its eclectic nature and the abundance of architectural styles. The city has examples of architecture spanning the centuries from the Middle Ages to the present day, including Gothic, French Renaissance, Classical Revival, Flamboyant, Belle Epoque, Art Nouveau, Art Deco, and post-modern styles. Paris is considered one of the most beautiful cities in Europe due to its architectural diversity and the way it seamlessly blends different styles across different epochs. Layers of history exist harmoniously in the city, from the medieval city and its monuments to the extraordinary boulevards built in the 19th century and the houses that have been built by the Modernist masters of the 20th century.
